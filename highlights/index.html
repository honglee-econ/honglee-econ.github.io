Research

 

"Market Power Transition and Resale Value Effect in the Secondary Market"
(Job Market Paper)

Presentations: Indiana University BEPP Seminar 2023 (Bloomington, IN)

Firms can benefit from increased resale values of their secondary products by facilitating the consumers to have a greater willingness to pay for a new product. How do direct-to-consumer sales of the secondary products affect demand for primary market goods?

"Market Power, Tacit Collusion, and Product Positioning by Family Firms"
(with Chun-Yu Ho and Jaehak Lee)

How do family firms find motivation to compete, and what impact does their tacit collusion have on product positioning and consumer welfare?

 

"From Cold-Start to Warm Reception: The Impact of Knowledge Contribution as a Signaling Mechanism"
(with Minsoo Park)

Presentations: Southern Economic Association 2023 (New Orleans, LA), Indiana University BEPP Seminar 2022 (Bloomington, IN)

Reputation systems can be beneficial for well-established firms, but can create entry barrier for entrants, which is referred to “cold-start problem”. Can this challenge be mitigated if qualified newcomers signal their quality in the cold period?

 

"Estimating the Economic Value of the Online Marketplace for Legal Services"
(with Minsoo Park and Jungmin Kim)

KDI Journal of Economic Policy, 2023, 45(3), 49-73, DOI: 10.23895/kdijep.2023.45.3.49

  • Available at SSRN: http://dx.doi.org/10.23895/kdijep.2023.45.3.49

Conflicts between traditional, offline service providers and new, online platforms are not uncommon across various industries: real estate agents vs. online platforms (Zillow, Redfin, Trulia); taxi drivers vs. ride-sharing apps (Uber, Lyft, Didi Chuxing), traditional travel agencies vs. online platforms (Expedia, Booking.com, Airbnb); traditional restaurants vs. food delivery platforms (Doordash, Grubhub, Uber Eats); and bar associations vs online legal services (LegalZoom, Rocket Lawyer, Avvo). What is the economic value of online platforms to consumer utility and how do they change the dynamics of industry?

 

 

Other Professional Works

In addition to academic works, I have contributed to other reports through economic consulting in antitrust cases with a district court in South Korea, the Construction Association of Korea, and DL E&C. Below is a summary of the projects.

Economic Appraisal in Civil Antitrust Litigation of Bid-rigging Case: When the plaintiff and dependent do not agree upon the damage amount in a civil bid-rigging litigation, the court appoints an economist to settle the case down. Appointed by the Suwon district court (the case number 2016가합101582), our economic analysis team was in charge of evaluating the overcharges caused by collusive coordination among water treatment companies in a public procurement auction. Web-scraping the universe data of the relevant constructions from the public and private procurement auctions, I estimated that the differential between the counterfactual winning bid under competition and the actual winning bid under collusion is 15.8%, and the results are robust under different identification strategies. 

Econometric Methods of Measuring the Bid-rigging Damages in Public Procurement Auctions: Commissioned by the Construction Association of Korea, this research report delineates various estimates of bid-rigging damages using numerous econometric models adopted by South Korea's federal courts for estimating the antitrust damages. The methods include difference-in-difference (DID), forecasting model, yardstick method, and before-and-after method. We apply these models to measure the bid-rigging damages in the Four Major Rivers Restoration Project in South Korea. The results reveal that yardstick and before-and-after methods fail to provide credible estimates than difference-in-difference (DID) because they do not fully capture the heterogenous characteristics across different constructions and intertemporal variations. 

An Economic Analysis Representative for DL E&C (the Defendant) in Antitrust Litigation Case: Our team conducts preliminary analysis on the estimation of damage compensations resulting from the collusive cooperation in several constructions.

 

Idea Developing Project

"Bloody Protectionism"
(solo work for now)

Protectionism refers to government policites designed to restrict international trade with the aim of safeguarding domestic industries against foreign competition. In pursuit of this objective, especially in developing countries, governments may overtly or implicitly endorse the establishment of monopolistic structures within the domestic market, intending to bolster the competitive capabilities of domestic companies on the global stage. However, this strategy is not without its costs, particularly to domestic consumers who experience a diminution of competitive forces. I seek to quantify the adverse impact of protectionist practices on consumers and evaluate the export changes on their competitiveness in the international market.
